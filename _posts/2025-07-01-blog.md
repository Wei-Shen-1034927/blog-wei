* Got a broken belt at Mt Hutt

It was a beautiful day with no wind, perfect for skiing. Got up, wake kids, cooked breakfast, and ready to head to Mt Hutt. 

I had to detour to pick up Beibei from Rolleston. As I was driving on SH73 I heard a squicking sound from the engine, I stoppped the car and the noise disappeared, so I kept on driving. Just about 20 minutes later, the noise reappeared but very small and doesn't sound like anything major. I kept on driving. When I started the Mt Hutt climb, the noise becomes distinctively louder, but still doesn't sound like anything major. Until we got to avalanche corner, the noise becomes extremely loud and suddenly the steering is not working and the battery light came on. I had to immediately stop the car on the oppersite side of the road and parked in a 45 degree angle to block half of the road. 

I quickly got off the car, and popped the bonnet. This is what i see: 

![a-broken-drive-belt](/blog-wei/asset/images/2025-07-01-image.jpeg)

** a broken drive belt

One of the Mt Hutt staff came to us, he gave me a number for Methven Motors, which operates the local towing services. So I called them and got my Subaru towed to Methven Motors. Queenie and Beibei went on to ski for half a day as the tow truck can only take two person with it. 

At Methven Motors, I was told that the belt is broken because one of the idling pulley is stuck and chewed the belt to pieces, so I need to replace a belt and the idling pulley. While waiting for the parts to arrive, I took the courtesy car to Ashburton and had MacDonalds for lunch. Later in the afternoon, I picked up Queenie and wondered around Methven until almost 5pm. 

In the end, the parts didn't arrive, so the lady mechanic replaced two seals and a belt for $107.
